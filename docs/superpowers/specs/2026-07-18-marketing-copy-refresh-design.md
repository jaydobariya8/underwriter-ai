# Marketing Copy Refresh Design

## Goal

Reposition Underwriter AI as an underwriting workflow product that helps analysts perform institutional-quality diligence, rather than as a generic credit-analysis tool or financial advisory brand.

## Scope

Update the existing marketing homepage without redesigning its established stone, navy, and muted-gold visual system. Preserve the current navigation, product mockup, section order, trust language, MVP disclosure, contact details, responsive behavior, and understated presentation.

## Content Design

### Hero

- Replace the current headline with: “Institutional-grade credit judgment, built into your diligence process.”
- Replace the supporting paragraph with copy explaining that Underwriter AI ingests deal data, surfaces the questions that matter, tests whether the transaction works, and prepares committee-ready credit materials through MD review.
- Preserve the existing eyebrow, calls to action, two-column composition, and product mockup.

### Institutional Judgment

- Replace the transaction-count claim with: “Institutional credit judgment shaped by more than a decade of hands-on underwriting experience and encoded into 35 analytical rules.”
- Keep “institutional credit judgment” and “35 analytical rules” visually emphasized.

### Product Capabilities

Retain the six-item responsive grid while broadening the capabilities to cover:

1. Ingesting and structuring transaction data.
2. Surfacing the right diligence questions.
3. Testing whether the transaction works.
4. Pressure-testing covenants and debt capacity.
5. Drafting evidence-led credit memos, including MD-ready versions.
6. Preparing IC briefings, scorecards, unresolved risks, and escalation paths.

### Proof

- Replace the “40+ transactions” proof point with “10+ years” of hands-on underwriting experience.
- Keep the existing proof points for 35 encoded analytical rules and three connected workflow stages.

### Trust

Leave “Trust starts with clear boundaries” and the current MVP disclosure unchanged.

### Team

Convert the single-founder content area into a restrained two-column grid:

- Arya Chudasama — Founder & CEO, former Goldman Sachs. State that he spent nearly five years at Goldman Sachs and brings hands-on leveraged-finance underwriting judgment to the product.
- Jay Dobariya — Co-founder & CTO. State that he built the technical stack that turns the underwriting methodology into a working product.

Do not add locations because a location was not provided for both founders.

### Final Call to Action

- Reframe the headline and paragraph around giving analysts their time back and reducing 16–20-hour underwriting grinds.
- Keep the pilot/contact action and New York label.
- Remove the demo phone number, its environment check, and its production-placeholder language entirely.

### Metadata

Broaden the marketing metadata description so search and social previews reflect the full diligence workflow rather than only live credit guidance.

## Visual Direction

Use the existing minimal visual language without introducing a new decorative motif. The only layout adjustment is the team grid, which will use the same gold hairlines, typography, spacing, and responsive breakpoints as the product capabilities. Longer copy must remain readable at mobile widths without changing the existing type system.

## Accessibility and Quality

- Preserve semantic heading order and list structure.
- Keep existing focus styles and responsive behavior.
- Avoid client-side state or new dependencies.
- Verify with lint and a production build.

## Out of Scope

- Changes to the application dashboard or underwriting workflow.
- Changes to the trust/MVP disclosure.
- New imagery, animation, fonts, colors, routes, or dependencies.
